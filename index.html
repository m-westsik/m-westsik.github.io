Previous Work

3D Printing Industrial Placement Student

Worked within the AM4Space research group at the UK Astronomy Technology Centre, collaborated with project scientists, mechanical design engineers and project managers. Designed a mirror petal, based on a CubeSat Earth Observation instrument, for Power Bed Fusion additive manufacturing. Was able to reduce the component sub-assembly's mass by 44% and consolidated nine parts into a single part. Conducted design analysis and wrote a conference paper summarizing the project which was orally presented by the project supervisor at the 2023 SPIE Optics + Photonics conference. Personally presented the work to multiple professional audiences, within the company and abroad. The presentation and publicaiton are available through this link.

Internet of Things (IoT) in Aerospace Engineering project

Designed an experimental rig for outreach using IoT and plastic additive manufacturing technologies. Developed a webserver for the controlin the experiments parameters and real time data visualization. For more information see the final report.

Conceptual Aerospace Systems Design project

As part of our Conceptual Aerospace Systems Design module, we were given the task to develop a Space Debris Removal mission concept. Within our team, I took the Mission Analyst position providing critical input to the team's work and shaping the mission concept from the very beginning. I analyzed ESA datasets for polluted orbits and types of pollution, wrote requirements, did research on launch vehicles and propulsion systems, and answered critical questions during the tradeshow presentation. Our team recieved the Best Tradeshow Presentation award, and was was given the highest mark out of all teams. For more information, read our final report.

Lead Mechanical Engineer of the AAS CanSat team

Led the development of the mechanical sub-system of the CanSat, in particular the conceptual and detailed design stages, manufacturing and assembly of the system. Performed system evel analysis and wrote extensive PDR and CDR documentation. Managed the work of other two students. The team participated in the final stage of the competition in Virginia, USA, and reached 15th overall position out of 50, the team's best result in the last 3 years.

Mechanical and Integration & Testing Engineer in the AAS CanSat Team

Designed numerous components and mechanisms in SolidWorks, performed Finite Element Analysis, 3D printed components and assembled the system. Led sub-system and system level integration, and supported the team in the final stage of the competiton.

Autonomous systems engineer

Worked within the autonomous systems group in the university's BAE Systems Drone Swarm Challenge team. Set up a system that was able to locate a target and provide its relative location using an Arduino board and a JeVois machine vision module.
